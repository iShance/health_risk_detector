Algorithms Used
σ
Logistic Regression Stable
Outputs a probability between 0–1 using the sigmoid function. A score of 0.82 means 82% confidence of risk. Stable across datasets, easy to interpret mathematically. Best for linear decision boundaries.
K
K-Nearest Neighbors Scale-sensitive
Finds the K most similar patients in training data. If 4 out of 5 neighbors are "at risk", predicts risk. Very sensitive to feature scaling — normalization is critical. Works well with small, well-distributed datasets.
🌿
Decision Tree Interpretable
Creates human-readable rules: IF BP > 140 → Risk, ELSE IF HR > 100 AND activity < 4 → Risk. Easiest to explain to non-technical users and medical staff. Can overfit on small datasets.

Limitations & Critical Thinking
Small synthetic dataset — not validated on real clinical data
Missing features: age, weight, smoking history, family history, cholesterol
KNN requires feature normalization — raw values can skew distances
Decision Tree may overfit with depth > 4 on small datasets
Never use as a replacement for professional medical diagnosis
